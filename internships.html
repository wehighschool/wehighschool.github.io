Opportunities for You

  • Google's Computer Science Summer Institute

    Cambridge, Chicago, Mountain View, New York and Seattle

    Google's Computer Science Summer Institute (CSSI) is a three-week introduction to computer science for graduating high school seniors with a passion for technology.It's an intensive, interactive, hands-on and fun program that seeks to inspire the tech leaders and innovators of tomorrow by supporting the study of computer science, software engineering and other closely related subjects.

    The application for the 2016 program is now CLOSED. Please check back in January 2017 for the 2017 application.

  • NASA

    Palo Alto, CA; Cleveland, OH; New York, NY; Greenbelt, MD; Pasadena, CA; Houston, TX; Kennedy Space Center, FL; Hampton, VA; Huntsville, AL; Washington D.C.; Stennis Space Center, MS; Wallops Island, VA; Las Cruces, NM

    NASA offers a wide variety of Internships and Fellowships, NASA Internships are educational hands-on opportunities that provide unique NASA-related research and operational experiences for high school, undergraduate and graduate students as well as educators.

    Fall internship applications are accepted: 03/02/2016 - 5/31/2016

    Spring internship applications are accepted: 06/02/2016 - 10/17/2016

    Summer internship applications are accepted: 11/10/2015 - 03/01/2016

    Year-long internship applications are accepted at any time.

  • Smithsonian

    Washington, DC

    The Smithsonian offers internship programs in a wide variety of fields encompassing business and public administration, history, art, science, culture, and education. They have a wide variety of opportunities that place interns throughout the Smithsonian. You can find specific programs offered by various units in your field of interest on their website.

    Each program in the Smithsonian has a different deadline application, you can find each one on their website.

  • Facebook Summer Academy

Externs will work closely with Facebook Mentors in their respective capacities to gain real-world work experience and on-the-job skills that will help propel them into successful careers.

  • National Institutes of Health

    Bethesda, MD; Baltimore and Frederick, MD; Research Triangle Park, NC; Hamilton, MT; Framingham, MA; Phoenix, AZ; Detroit, MI.

    The wide variety of summer programs at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) provide an opportunity to spend a summer working at the NIH side-by-side with some of the leading scientists in the world, in an environment devoted exclusively to biomedical research. You can find all of these programs on their website.

    The application for the 2016 program is now CLOSED. The application for summer 2017 will be available in mid-November.

  • The United States Intelligence Community- Intelligence Careers

    Ft. Meade, MD

    The IC offers students a wide variety of excellent programs to get a jumpstart on a career in intelligence. You can choose programs from the Defense Intelligence Agency, the National Security Agency or the National Geospatial-Intel Agency; whether you're a High School student, a College student or a College Graduate. Intelligence Careers offers Internships, Fellowships, Scholarships, Co-ops and Work Studies in every Academic Field.

    Most applications begin to be accepted on Sept. 1st of every year, depending on which program you're interested in.

  • Northrop Grumman Careers

    Redondo Beach, CA; Linthicum, Maryland; Herndon, VA

    The Northrop Grumman HIP program encourages and advances the pursuit of technical degrees by providing hands-on experiences and improving access to postsecondary education. The program provides high school seniors with school credit and on-the-job experience (not available at all locations). You'll be on site for approximately 8 weeks during the summer learning about Northrop Grumman and gaining real world work experience

    The application for the 2016 program is now CLOSED.

  • U.S. Department of Education

    Cambridge, Chicago, Mountain View, New York and Seattle

    The Department of Education (ED) offers internships for students interested in seeking valuable work experience in government and federal education policy and administration. Volunteers have the opportunity to make meaningful contributions to the Department's mission to promote student achievement and preparation for global competitiveness by fostering educational excellence and ensuring equal access. ED offers internships in the fall, winter/spring, and summer. Typically interns are with the Department for eight to ten weeks.

    For the Fall internship, the deadline is JULY 15. For Winter/Spring, the deadline is OCTOBER 1. For Summer, the deadline is MARCH 15.

  • U.S. Office of Personal Management

    Cambridge, Chicago, Mountain View, New York and Seattle

    This Program is designed to provide students enrolled in a wide variety of educational institutions, from high school to graduate level, with opportunities to work in agencies and explore Federal careers while still in school and while getting paid for the work performed. Students who successfully complete the program may be eligible for conversion to a permanent job in the civil service.

    OPM has a wide variety of internship programs and each one has it's own submission deadline.

  • Science and Engineering Apprenticeship Program

    Cambridge, Chicago, Mountain View, New York and Seattle

    This program is designed to encourage students to pursue science and engineering careers; SEAP places academically talented high school students with interest and ability in science and mathematics as apprentices in Department of Navy (DoN) laboratories for eight weeks during the summer. These students work with scientists and engineers who act as mentors. The program offers students a unique and positive experience in their fields of interest, thus encouraging them to pursue careers in science and engineering.

    The application for the 2016 program is now CLOSED.

  • Office of Science and Technology Policy

    Cambridge, Chicago, Mountain View, New York and Seattle

    Serving as an OSTP Intern provides a unique opportunity to work closely with senior White House officials and science and technology (S&T) policy analysts in OSTP's topic-based divisions (Division internship), or on OSTP's legal team (Legal internship). Applicants are encouraged to apply for one of three terms (Fall, Spring, or Summer), each term lasting no more than 90 days.

    The OSTP deadlines depend on your interest, whether you want a Policy Internship or Legal Internship.

  • U.S. Department of Energy Summer Internships

    Cambridge, Chicago, Mountain View, New York and Seattle

    This program at Fermilab is a highly competitive, paid, six-week summer internship opportunity for ILLINOIS high school sophomores and juniors with demonstrated aptitude and strong interest in physics, mathematics, computer science and engineering. The program's goals are to encourage high school students to undertake college study and pursue careers in S.T.E.M. disciplines.

    This program has many subprograms, each with it's own submission deadline.
